Appliances are essential tools that make our daily lives more convenient, but they can also experience problems from time to time. Understanding common appliance issues can help you identify when to seek professional assistance. Here are some of the most frequent problems that homeowners encounter with their appliances:

Refregarator

Not Cooling

One of the most common issues with refrigerators is when they fail to maintain the desired temperature, resulting in spoiled food. This could be due to a malfunctioning thermostat, compressor, or evaporator fan.

Book Now

Leaking Water

Leaks from the refrigerator can be caused by a clogged defrost drain, damaged water line, or faulty door seals. Addressing leaks promptly is essential to prevent water damage to your kitchen floor and surrounding areas.

Book Now

Washing Machine

Not Draining

A washing machine that fails to drain properly may have a clogged drain hose, faulty pump, or malfunctioning lid switch. This can result in standing water in the tub and clothes that are not properly cleaned.

Book Now

Shaking or Vibrating

Excessive shaking or vibrating during the spin cycle is often caused by an unbalanced load or uneven flooring. However, it can also indicate a problem with the machine's suspension springs or shock absorbers.

Book Now

Dishwasher

Not Cleaning Dishes Properly

If your dishwasher is leaving dishes dirty or streaky, it could be due to clogged spray arms, a worn-out pump, or insufficient water pressure. Regular cleaning and maintenance can help prevent this issue.

Book Now

Not Draining Completely

A dishwasher that doesn't drain completely may have a clogged drain hose, faulty drain pump, or malfunctioning drain valve. This can lead to foul odors and water damage to the kitchen cabinets

Book Now

Oven and Range

Uneven Heating

An oven or range that heats unevenly may have a faulty heating element, temperature sensor, or igniter. This can result in food that is overcooked in some areas and undercooked in others.

Book Now

Gas Smell

If you detect a gas smell coming from your gas oven or range, it could indicate a gas leak, which is a serious safety hazard. Turn off the appliance immediately and contact a professional for inspection and repair.

Book Now

Dryer

Not Heating

A dryer that fails to produce heat may have a faulty heating element, thermostat, or thermal fuse. This can result in damp or wrinkled clothes, prolonging the drying process.

Book Now

Overheating

Overheating dryers can be caused by blocked vents, damaged heating elements, or malfunctioning thermostats. Addressing this issue promptly is crucial to prevent fire hazards.
